Whitewash & Co. HR Manual

Version 1.0 (Foundational Draft)

1. Introduction
1.1 Purpose

This HR Manual establishes guidelines, expectations, and practices that support employees while preserving the company’s core philosophy:

“If you can’t fix the system, make the system think it fixed itself.”

1.2 Scope

Applies to all employees, contractors, and affiliates of Whitewash & Co.

1.3 Guiding Principles
Creativity over rigid process
Outcomes over optics
Ethical mischief (no harm, no illegality)
Adaptability over hierarchy
2. Employment Philosophy
2.1 At-Will Employment

All employment is at-will unless otherwise specified in writing.

2.2 Equal Opportunity

Whitewash & Co. provides equal employment opportunities regardless of legally protected characteristics.

2.3 Culture Fit

Employees are expected to:

Think unconventionally
Operate comfortably in ambiguity
Solve problems indirectly when appropriate
Maintain discretion and plausible deniability
3. Code of Conduct
3.1 Professional Behavior

While creativity is encouraged, employees must:

Act lawfully at all times
Avoid deception that causes harm
Maintain client confidentiality
Respect colleagues and clients
3.2 Ethical Boundaries

Permitted:

Behavioral nudging
Incentive design
Narrative framing

Not permitted:

Fraud or misrepresentation causing harm
Coercion or manipulation without consent
Violations of law or regulation
3.3 Confidentiality

Employees must not disclose:

Client identities (unless authorized)
Methods, strategies, or frameworks
Internal operational practices
4. Work Structure
4.1 Organizational Design

Whitewash & Co. operates with intentional fluidity:

Minimal rigid hierarchy
Project-based leadership
Dynamic team formation
4.2 Roles & Responsibilities

Roles may shift based on:

Project needs
Individual strengths
Strategic priorities
4.3 Decision-Making
Decisions should be made at the lowest effective level
Speed is prioritized over perfection
Creative solutions are encouraged
5. Work Hours & Flexibility
5.1 Work Schedule
Flexible working hours
Employees are responsible for delivering results
5.2 Remote Work
Remote-first environment
Employees must maintain availability for key collaboration periods
5.3 Time Off
Flexible PTO policy (manager-approved)
Encouraged to prevent burnout
6. Performance Management
6.1 Performance Philosophy

Performance is measured by:

Outcomes achieved
Creativity of approach
Client impact
Ability to navigate ambiguity
6.2 Feedback
Continuous and informal
Peer-driven where appropriate
Formal reviews conducted periodically
6.3 Recognition

Employees may be recognized for:

Innovative problem-solving
Successful “invisible wins”
Turning liabilities into assets
7. Compensation & Benefits
7.1 Compensation
Market-aligned base pay
Performance-based bonuses tied to outcomes
7.2 Benefits
Health and wellness offerings (as applicable)
Flexible work arrangements
Professional development opportunities
8. Training & Development
8.1 Onboarding

New hires will be introduced to:

Company philosophy
Core methods (e.g., “Tom Sawyer Method™”)
Ethical boundaries
8.2 Ongoing Development
Skill-building in psychology, negotiation, and strategy
Cross-functional exposure
Experimentation encouraged
9. Workplace Policies
9.1 Communication
Clear, concise, and intentional
Use of discretion when discussing sensitive matters
9.2 Conflict Resolution
Reframe before escalating
Seek creative, non-traditional solutions
Escalate only when necessary
9.3 Use of Company Resources
Must support business objectives
Avoid unnecessary bureaucracy
10. Compliance & Risk
10.1 Legal Compliance

All employees must comply with:

Applicable laws and regulations
Client contractual obligations
10.2 Risk Awareness

Employees should:

Evaluate unintended consequences
Avoid reputational harm
Escalate uncertain situations
11. Separation of Employment
11.1 Voluntary Separation

Employees are encouraged to provide notice.

11.2 Involuntary Separation

May occur due to:

Performance issues
Policy violations
Business needs
11.3 Exit Obligations
Return company property
Maintain confidentiality post-employment
12. Acknowledgment

Employees must acknowledge that they:

Have read and understood this manual
Agree to comply with company policies
